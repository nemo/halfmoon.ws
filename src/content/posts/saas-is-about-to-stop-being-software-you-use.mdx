You don't use a spreadsheet. You think _in_ it.

The next wave of software isn't about features. It's about that — thinking _in_ it. Software that moves when you move. That listens, executes, and adapts without you ever clicking through a settings panel.

Right now, most SaaS is still 2015 with a chatbot bolted on. You buy a tool. You learn its taxonomy. You configure workflows that reflect how the software thinks, not how you think.

That's ending.

The real shift: the product surface is a conversation. Behind it, a fleet of specialized agents — each with their own environment — actually doing the work. Building dashboards. Running experiments. Shipping to ad platforms. Modifying code. Deploying.

You say what you want. They show their work. You steer.

Not "AI assistance." An operating system you co-create — one that changes itself based on what you're trying to accomplish.

The old loop: _buy software → adapt your business to it → suffer._

The new loop: _state intent → agents execute → review and steer → system evolves._

Marketing, sales, finance, product, legal — every domain will have its own agent environment. Domain knowledge built in. Memory that accumulates. Infrastructure tuned to the work.

Software that never loses context. Never forgets the thread. Never makes you re-explain yourself.

That's not a feature. That's a new category.
